User Personas

Four primary personas identified for the Design Dashboard project. Each represents distinct jobs-to-be-done within the design workflow.

S

Sarah Chen

Lead Product Designer • 6 years experience

Dashboard Job-to-be-Done
"When I complete design work, I want to showcase it in a portfolio-like dashboard so that stakeholders can see my design thinking and process without me having to explain it in every meeting."
Key Pain Points
  • Spends 4-6 hours/week in meetings explaining the same design decisions
  • Stakeholders ask "why did you choose this?" but deliverables don't show rationale
  • Design work scattered across Figma, Slack, Google Docs - no single source of truth
  • Hard to demonstrate design progression from research → concepts → production
Goals for Dashboard
  • Reduce repeat explanations by making design rationale visible and accessible
  • Build credibility by showing research-backed decision making
  • Create a portfolio of work that tells the story of each project
M

Marcus Rodriguez

Product Manager • 8 years experience

Dashboard Job-to-be-Done
"When I need to understand design status and decisions, I want quick access to design rationale and deliverables so that I can update stakeholders and make informed product decisions without interrupting designers."
Key Pain Points
  • Can't answer exec questions about design without pinging Sarah
  • No visibility into design progress - "Is that mockup final?"
  • Struggles to connect design decisions back to user needs
  • Needs design context for sprint planning and backlog prioritization
Goals for Dashboard
  • Self-service access to design status and rationale
  • Understand which designs are concepts vs. production-ready
  • See how design connects to user research and business goals

Summary

All four personas share a common pain: design work is invisible and inaccessible. The dashboard must make design thinking visible, accessible, and self-service.

"I spend more time explaining my design work than actually doing design work. If stakeholders could just see the context and rationale, I'd get hours back every week."
— Sarah Chen, Lead Designer
